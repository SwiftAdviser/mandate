## What They Are

"The Control Center for Agentic Payments." Enterprise-focused infrastructure for AI agent payment governance.

**Product:**
- Agent wallet + spending management with configurable limits
- Policy enforcement at transaction execution time
- Relationship tracking between agents and counterparties
- Real-time visibility + audit trails
- SDK, REST API, webhooks
- Virtual cards + stablecoin payments

**Target ICPs (three buyer personas):**
1. **Developers** — building agentic systems
2. **Finance & Ops teams** — spend reporting and reconciliation
3. **Security & Compliance teams** — policy enforcement, threat detection

## Honest Assessment

**Why Conto sounds powerful:**
- "Control Center" framing is clean and enterprise-ready
- Finance & Ops personas = budget holders (not just devs)
- "Network-wide threat intelligence" sounds like a moat (even if unbuilt)

**Why they may not have users:**
- Enterprise sales cycle = 6-12 months minimum
- Finance/Compliance buyer = longest approval chain
- Waitlist = no GTM shortcut yet
- "Virtual cards" implies fiat infra partnership needed — not built yet

**What Mandate has that Conto doesn't:**
1. Non-custodial architecture — fundamental trust advantage
2. Live integrations (10 frameworks) — developers can use it TODAY
3. ERC-8004/8183 alignment — becoming the standard, not a vendor product
4. Crypto-native (stablecoins, on-chain policies) vs Conto hedging toward fiat

---

## Strategic Implication

Conto is going enterprise top-down. Mandate can win developer bottom-up — like Stripe did to enterprise payment processors.

The niche question: **who builds the most agent wallets in the next 12 months?**
- Not enterprise Finance teams (they don't write code)
- It's the developers building agent products for those Finance teams

Mandate's path: own the developer layer → enterprise adoption follows through their customers.
